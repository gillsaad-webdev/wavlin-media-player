About Artist

Gur Sidhu, born Gursimran Singh Sidhu on July 11, 1997, in Chak Fateh Singh Wala, Bathinda, Punjab, is a prominent Indian-Canadian singer, songwriter, and record producer known for his contributions to Punjabi music and cinema. His father, Sukhbir Singh Sidhu, was also a Punjabi singer, which influenced Gursimran's early interest in music. He began his musical journey with the single "Sarkaare" in 2016, which carried a political message. His breakthrough came in 2019 with the release of "Bro Oye" under White Hill Music. He went on to collaborate with artists like Baani Sandhu , Jasmine Sandlas , and Cheema Y, delivering hits such as "8 Parche," "Vaddi Galbaat," "Bamb Aagya," and "California Love".
His debut album, Nothing Like Before, released in 2021, featured tracks such as "Taakre," "Dekhi Jau," and "Ashke Ashke," showcasing his versatility. Beyond singing, he has also produced music for artists like Jassa Dhillon , Ranjit Bawa, Bohemia, and Gulab Sidhu. Known for blending Bhangra, Hip-Hop, and Pop, Gur Sidhu remains a popular figure in modern Punjabi music !
